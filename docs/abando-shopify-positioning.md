## Short App Store Description

## Long App Store Description

Abando helps Shopify merchants understand where checkout revenue may be leaking before install, then confirm the real issue after connection.

Abando starts with a free pre-install checkout audit and scorecard. That scorecard uses benchmark patterns to estimate where a store may be losing momentum between cart, checkout, payment, and purchase.

After connecting Shopify, Abando moves from estimate to confirmation. It begins tracking checkout behavior so merchants can see which issue is surfacing most clearly, where customers may be hesitating, and what to review first.

Abando is designed for merchants who want a simpler answer to one question:

Where are we most likely losing customers in checkout, and what should we check first?

What Abando does well today:

- Free pre-install audit for fast checkout diagnosis
- Merchant-readable scorecard and issue framing
- Post-install dashboard that confirms connected-store status
- Early tracked signals tied to the main checkout issue
- Clear next-step recommendations for what to review first

What Abando does not claim:

- It does not claim exact live revenue loss before install
- It does not pretend benchmark estimates are tracked metrics
- It does not claim heatmaps, session replay, or recovery automation unless those capabilities are actually built

Abando is best understood as a checkout diagnosis and confirmation layer for Shopify:

1. Run the free audit
2. Review the scorecard estimate
3. Connect Shopify
4. Confirm the issue with tracked checkout behavior

## Top 5 Feature Bullets

1. Free pre-install checkout audit for Shopify stores
2. Benchmark-based scorecard that highlights the clearest checkout issue
3. Merchant-readable explanation of where customers may be hesitating
4. Post-install dashboard with connected-store and tracking status
5. One primary issue and one recommended action to review first

## Top 5 Trust and Accuracy Guardrails

1. Before install, Abando shows a benchmark-based estimate, not tracked store revenue.
2. After install, Abando begins confirming issues with tracked checkout behavior.
3. Early post-install signals may strengthen, soften, or change as more activity is collected.
4. Abando should not claim heatmaps, replay, or exact telemetry unless those systems are truly live.
5. Merchants should always understand the difference between diagnosis before install and confirmation after install.

## What Abando Is Not

Abando is not:

- an ERP
- a full customer support suite
- a generic heatmap or replay platform
- an abandoned-cart email tool only
- a broad business intelligence dashboard

Abando is specifically about checkout revenue leakage detection and confirmation for Shopify.

## Comparison Angle Versus Generic Analytics Apps

Generic analytics apps usually show broader store data, many metrics, and many reports. Abando’s wedge is narrower and more actionable:

- It starts with a free checkout-specific diagnosis before install.
- It focuses on one likely issue instead of flooding the merchant with dashboards.
- It ties the scorecard to a merchant-readable explanation of why that issue may matter.
- It then uses post-install tracking to confirm whether the same pattern is really happening.

Comparison framing:

Generic analytics apps tell you more about everything.

Abando is built to tell you where checkout may be leaking revenue, what to check first, and whether that issue is confirmed after install.

### Positioning Rule

App Store messaging should lean into:

- diagnosis before install
- confirmation after install
- checkout revenue leakage detection

It should avoid claiming:

- exact pre-install telemetry
- mature replay/heatmap features
- broad BI or lifecycle automation scope that the product does not yet support
