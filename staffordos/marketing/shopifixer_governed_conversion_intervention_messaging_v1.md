## Core Definition

A ShopiFixer Fix Sprint is a **Governed Conversion Intervention**:

> One prioritized customer-journey problem addressed through a bounded group of
> coordinated storefront changes, including diagnosis, implementation, QA,
> before/after evidence, rollback protection, proof package, and measurement plan.

This is the same $950 Fix Sprint, described more accurately and more strongly.

---

## Why This Positioning Is Stronger

The old framing ("we fix one visible issue") undersold the discipline behind the
work. The Governed Conversion Intervention framing names what the merchant is
actually buying:

- **Prioritized** — the one problem chosen is the one most worth solving first.
- **Customer-journey problem** — a friction point in how shoppers move through the
  store, not a cosmetic defect.
- **Bounded group of coordinated changes** — resolving a journey problem often
  takes several changes that must work together; they are scoped and bounded.
- **Governed** — diagnosis → implementation → QA → before/after evidence →
  rollback protection → proof package → measurement plan. Every step is evidenced.

The strength is in the governance, not in bigger promises.

---

## What Stays Exactly The Same

This positioning is a language upgrade only. It preserves, without exception:

- **$950 flat fee** — unchanged.
- **One scoped problem** — one prioritized customer-journey problem per sprint.
- **No full redesign** — a bounded group of coordinated changes, never a rebuild.
- **No retainer** — one-time engagement, not ongoing.
- **No guaranteed revenue** — a measurement plan is included; no revenue or
  conversion outcome is promised.
- **No unbounded revisions** — scope is bounded; revisions are not unlimited.
- **ShopiFixer and Abando remain separate products** — never bundled or merged.

## Claim Discipline (must / must-not)

**May say:**

- "One prioritized customer-journey problem."
- "A bounded group of coordinated storefront changes."
- "Diagnosed, implemented, QA'd, evidenced, rollback-protected, and proven."
- "Includes a measurement plan so you can see whether it worked."
- "$950 flat fee."

**Must NOT say:**

- Any promised revenue lift, conversion-rate increase, or ROI figure.
- "Full redesign," "rebuild," "overhaul," or "unlimited changes."
- "Retainer," "ongoing," "monthly," or "subscription" (that is Abando's model, a
  separate product).
- Anything that bundles ShopiFixer with Abando or implies one includes the other.
- Any specific numeric outcome not yet produced (no invented before/after numbers).

---

## Measurement Plan, Not Outcome Guarantee

The measurement plan is a deliverable, not a promise. It tells the merchant **how**
the resolved journey problem will be observed — which signals to watch and how
before/after evidence is compared. It never states a guaranteed conversion or
revenue result. Attribution and revenue reporting remain Finance authority per the
marketing operating architecture; marketing messaging never reports estimates as
revenue.

---

## Product Separation

ShopiFixer (a one-time, $950 Governed Conversion Intervention service) and Abando
(a separate SaaS subscription) are distinct products. Messaging may seed
post-sprint interest in Abando, but must never merge the offers or imply the Fix
Sprint includes Abando. Product routing occurs after diagnosis, based on evidence.

---

## Anti-Drift Rule

The merchant buys one Governed Conversion Intervention: one prioritized journey
problem, a bounded group of coordinated changes, at a flat $950. Do not expand
scope, promise outcomes, introduce a retainer, or bundle Abando. Stronger
positioning, same bounded product.
