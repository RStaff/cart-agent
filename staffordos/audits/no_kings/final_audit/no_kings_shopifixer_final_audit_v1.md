# No Kings Athletics ShopiFixer Audit

## 1. Executive Summary

Store reviewed: No Kings Athletics

Overall observation: No Kings Athletics has a clear brand identity and a strong visual first impression. The homepage communicates attitude and positioning quickly, but it does not guide a first-time visitor toward a clear product decision soon enough.

Primary opportunity: Improve homepage product discovery and product value clarity. A visitor should understand what to buy, why it matters, and where to click before the page becomes mostly brand story.

Recommended sprint: Homepage Product Discovery and Value Clarity Fix Sprint.

This is a focused ShopiFixer sprint, not a redesign. The goal is to preserve the No Kings brand while making the first buying path clearer on desktop and mobile.

## 2. Assessment Scorecard

Trust: 6/10

The store appears legitimate and has trust cues lower on the page, including secure checkout and support information. The issue is placement. Purchase reassurance does not support the first product decision early enough.

Product Clarity: 5/10

The brand theme is clear. The product offer is less clear. A visitor can understand the attitude of the brand before they understand the primary product, best-selling item, product difference, or reason to buy now.

Product Discovery: 4/10

Product discovery is delayed. The homepage prioritizes brand expression first, then shows products later. The first product area is not strong enough to clearly guide the visitor into shopping.

Checkout Readiness: 5/10

The site appears functional, but the path from homepage interest to product selection and purchase is not emphasized strongly enough. The buying journey requires extra effort.

Mobile Experience: 6/10

The mobile layout works, but product value and next-action clarity are still delayed. A mobile visitor must scroll before the product offer becomes clear.

## 3. Findings Inventory

Finding 1: Homepage hero dominates first impression

Severity: High

Why it matters: The hero establishes the brand, but it delays the product decision. A first-time visitor sees the message before they clearly see what to buy.

Business impact: Product discovery is delayed, which can reduce the number of visitors who continue into product browsing.

Finding 2: Best-selling products are not highlighted early

Severity: High

Why it matters: The homepage does not quickly point the visitor toward the strongest product or collection. The first product section appears weak and does not carry the sale.

Business impact: Lower product engagement. Visitors may browse less because there is no obvious first product path.

Finding 3: Trust signals appear too late

Severity: Medium

Why it matters: Purchase reassurance exists lower on the page, but it does not support the first product decision. Trust should be visible near the moment a visitor is deciding whether to click into a product.

Business impact: Reduced buyer confidence during the early shopping path.

Finding 4: Navigation does not clearly direct first-time buyers

Severity: Medium

Why it matters: The navigation is simple, but it does not create an obvious first-time shopper path. The visitor has to decide where to go without much product guidance.

Business impact: More browsing friction and fewer clear product clicks.

Finding 5: Brand story outweighs commercial message

Severity: Medium

Why it matters: The brand message is strong, but the homepage needs to balance identity with buying guidance. A visitor should not have to choose between understanding the brand and finding the product.

Business impact: Reduced purchase intent because the commercial message arrives too late.

## 4. Recommended Fix Sprint

Selected issue: Homepage Product Discovery and Value Clarity.

Why selected: This issue is visible, explainable, fixable, and provable. It can be shown in before screenshots, improved with a scoped homepage change, and validated with after screenshots.

Why now: This is the clearest opportunity to improve the homepage without redesigning the store. The homepage already has brand strength. The next improvement should help that brand strength turn into a clearer product path.

Why not the other findings: The other findings matter, but they connect back to the same main issue. Trust placement, first-time navigation, product emphasis, and commercial clarity all improve when the homepage creates one clearer early buying path. A wider redesign or multi-page project is not needed for this sprint.

## 6. Proposed After State

This is the proposed outcome. It has not yet been implemented.

The homepage should keep the existing No Kings brand hero, but add a clearer product-value block directly below it.

The new buying path should answer four questions quickly:

- What is being sold?
- Why should this visitor care?
- What should they click first?
- Can they trust the path forward?

Recommended product-value block:

Headline: `Training gear for the standard you keep.`

Supporting line: `Start with the AirL Fabric Tee: lightweight daily training gear built around work, honor, and legacy.`

Primary CTA: `Shop the AirL Fabric Tee`

Secondary CTA: `View all gear`

Trust support: `Secure checkout. Store support available.`

The section should feature the primary product or primary collection with a real product image, correct title, correct price, and working link. On mobile, this block should appear before the `WORK / HONOR / LEGACY` content so the first shopping path is clear earlier.

Unsupported claims should not be added. Do not claim fast shipping, guaranteed returns, customer reviews, performance improvement, or conversion lift unless there is evidence to support those claims.

## 8. QA Plan

Desktop validation: Confirm the homepage loads correctly, the hero remains intact, the new product-value block appears directly below the hero, and the layout does not break.

Mobile validation: Confirm the product-value block appears early enough on mobile and remains readable without disrupting the existing brand presentation.

CTA validation: Confirm the primary CTA opens the intended product or collection, and the secondary CTA opens the intended broader shopping path.

Product validation: Confirm the product image, title, price, and link are real and correct. No `$0.00` price should appear unless the product is intentionally free.

Trust validation: Confirm every trust statement is supported by the store. Secure checkout and support can be used if they remain true. Shipping, returns, guarantees, reviews, or performance claims should only be used if verified.
