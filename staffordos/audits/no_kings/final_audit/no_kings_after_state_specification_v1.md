# No Kings After State Specification V1

## 1. Executive Summary

The goal of this ShopiFixer sprint is to make the No Kings Athletics homepage easier to shop without redesigning the store.

The current homepage has a strong brand opening, but the first clear buying path arrives too late and is not strong enough. After the sprint, a visitor should still feel the No Kings brand immediately, then quickly understand what product to look at first, why it matters, where to click, and why the buying path is trustworthy.

The sprint outcome is a clearer homepage product path, not a new brand, not a new store, and not a conversion guarantee.

## 2. Before State Summary

What the visitor currently sees:

- A strong hero section with the No Kings brand message.
- A hero CTA that says `ENTER THE FORGE`.
- A product area after the hero.
- Brand values: `WORK`, `HONOR`, and `LEGACY`.
- A featured products section farther down the page.
- Trust and support information lower on the homepage.

Where friction occurs:

- The first impression is brand-heavy.
- Product discovery and product value are delayed.
- The visitor does not immediately see what specific product to start with.
- The buying path is not clear enough near the first product decision.
- Trust support appears too late to help the visitor decide whether to continue shopping.

Why product discovery is weak:

- The homepage prioritizes brand expression over product navigation.
- The primary product is not presented with enough commercial clarity.
- The visitor has to scroll and interpret the page before understanding what to buy.
- The current first product area does not clearly communicate product value, product priority, or purchase confidence.

## 3. After State Narrative

After the sprint, the visitor journey should feel simple:

First, the visitor sees the existing No Kings hero and understands the brand position.

Second, immediately below the hero, the visitor sees a product-value section that presents the primary shopping path.

The visitor understands sooner:

- what product or collection to start with
- why the product is connected to the No Kings brand
- where to click next
- that checkout and support are available

The buying path becomes clearer because the homepage moves from brand impression directly into a concrete shopping decision. The visitor no longer has to search for the first product path or guess what the store wants them to do next.

## 4. Homepage Layout Specification

Recommended homepage order:

1. Header
2. Hero
3. NEW Product Value Section
4. Trust Section
5. Featured Products
6. Brand Story
7. Email Capture
8. Footer

Header purpose:

Keep basic navigation available. Header should remain simple and unchanged unless a link is broken.

Hero purpose:

Preserve the No Kings brand first impression. The hero should still communicate the brand world and attitude. Do not turn the hero into a generic sales banner.

NEW Product Value Section purpose:

Create the first clear shopping decision immediately after the hero. This section should show the primary product or collection, explain its value in plain language, and give the visitor a direct next click.

Trust Section purpose:

Move purchase reassurance closer to the first product decision. This should be short, factual, and verified.

Featured Products purpose:

Support browsing after the primary product path is clear. Featured products should not contain placeholder titles, placeholder images, or unclear prices.

Brand Story purpose:

Reinforce the No Kings identity after the visitor has already seen a clear product path. Brand story should support the sale, not delay it.

Email Capture purpose:

Give interested visitors a secondary action after they have seen the product path and brand story.

Footer purpose:

Provide standard store support, policy, and navigation information.

## 5. Product Value Section Specification

This is the centerpiece of the sprint.

Placement:

- Directly below the hero.
- Above `WORK / HONOR / LEGACY`.
- Above the broader featured products grid.

Recommended headline:

`Training gear for the standard you keep.`

Recommended subheadline:

`Start with the No Kings Athletics AirL Fabric Tee, the clearest first step into No Kings training gear.`

Primary CTA:

`Shop the AirL Fabric Tee`

Primary CTA destination:

- The AirL Fabric Tee product page, if that is the selected primary product.
- If the merchant chooses a collection instead, the CTA should point to the selected primary collection and the CTA should be renamed to match.

Secondary CTA:

`View all gear`

Secondary CTA destination:

- The main catalog or selected collection page.

Trust microcopy:

`Secure checkout. Store support available.`

Product image requirements:

- Use a real product image from Shopify.
- Do not use a blank gray product area.
- Do not use a generic placeholder illustration.
- The product image should match the product linked by the primary CTA.
- The image should be large enough to recognize the product on desktop and mobile.

Product title requirements:

- Use the exact Shopify product title.
- Do not show generic text such as `Product title`.
- If the product title is too long for mobile, it may wrap naturally but should not be truncated in a way that hides the product identity.

Product price requirements:

- Show the correct Shopify price.
- Do not show `$0.00` unless the product is intentionally free.
- If price is not ready, do not publish the after state.

Collection requirements:

- If a collection is featured, it should contain real products only.
- No placeholder products should appear in the first commercial section.
- Collection title should be clear, for example `Shop Training Gear` or `Featured No Kings Gear`.
- The collection path should lead toward product selection, not back into brand story.

Allowed section structure:

- Left side: headline, subheadline, trust microcopy, CTAs.
- Right side: featured product image, product title, price, product link.

Mobile structure:

- Headline first.
- Subheadline second.
- Product image third.
- Product title and price fourth.
- Primary CTA fifth.
- Secondary CTA sixth.
- Trust microcopy directly under the CTAs.

## 6. Mobile Layout Specification

Above the fold:

- Header.
- Hero brand message.
- Existing hero CTA if retained.

Immediately after the hero:

- Product Value Section headline.
- Product image or product card.
- Product title and price.
- Primary CTA.
- Trust microcopy.

What must remain visible:

- The first product path must be visible before the page moves into brand values or long brand story.
- The CTA must be readable and tappable.
- Product title and price must be clear.
- Trust microcopy must remain close to the CTA.

What should be moved lower:

- `WORK / HONOR / LEGACY`
- Broad featured product grids
- Long brand story
- Email capture

Mobile goal:

A mobile visitor should not have to scroll through brand values and broad product grids before understanding the first recommended shopping path.

## 7. Trust Placement Specification

Trust should appear in two places:

1. Inside or directly below the Product Value Section, next to the first buying decision.
2. In the existing lower trust/support area, if the store already uses it.

Allowed trust statements:

- `Secure checkout`
- `Store support available`
- `Support: support@nokingsathletics.com`, if this remains the correct support address.
- `Shopify Payments + PayPal`, if those payment options are active and visible.
- `Hands-off production and tracking`, if that existing statement remains true and approved by the merchant.

Forbidden trust statements unless separately verified:

- fast shipping
- free shipping
- guaranteed delivery dates
- easy returns
- money-back guarantee
- customer reviews
- five-star ratings
- made in USA
- premium material claims
- athletic performance improvement
- conversion improvement
- sales lift

Trust rule:

If the store cannot prove it, do not put it in the after state.

## 8. Merchant Proof Standard

The proof must be visual.

Before screenshot must show:

- The hero appears before a clear product decision.
- Product discovery is delayed or weak.
- The first product path is not clearly supported by product value and trust.
- Trust support appears lower than the first product decision.

After screenshot must show:

- The existing hero is preserved.
- A new product-value section appears directly below the hero.
- A real product image, product title, product price, and CTA are visible earlier.
- Trust microcopy appears near the first buying decision.
- Mobile layout makes the first product path clearer before deeper scrolling.

Before vs After comparison must show:

- Product value moved earlier.
- Buying path became more explicit.
- Trust moved closer to the decision point.
- Brand identity remained intact.
- The change is visible without relying on analytics or revenue claims.

## 9. QA Acceptance Criteria

Desktop:

- PASS: Header remains intact.
- PASS: Hero remains intact.
- PASS: Product Value Section appears directly below hero.
- PASS: Product image, title, price, and CTA are visible without layout break.
- FAIL: Product Value Section appears below brand values or broad featured products.
- FAIL: Layout breaks, overlaps, or hides product information.

Mobile:

- PASS: Hero remains intact.
- PASS: Product Value Section appears immediately after hero.
- PASS: CTA is readable and tappable.
- PASS: Product title and price are clear.
- FAIL: Visitor must scroll through `WORK / HONOR / LEGACY` before seeing the first product path.
- FAIL: Product card, CTA, or trust microcopy is cramped, hidden, or unreadable.

CTA:

- PASS: Primary CTA opens the selected product or collection.
- PASS: Secondary CTA opens the catalog or selected collection.
- FAIL: CTA leads to the wrong page, a broken page, or a vague non-shopping destination.

Product:

- PASS: Product image is real.
- PASS: Product title is exact.
- PASS: Product price is correct.
- PASS: Product link works.
- FAIL: Product shows `$0.00` unintentionally.
- FAIL: Product shows placeholder title or placeholder image.

Trust:

- PASS: Trust copy uses verified statements only.
- PASS: Trust copy appears near the first product decision.
- FAIL: Trust copy includes unsupported claims.
- FAIL: Trust appears only near the bottom of the page.

Buying Path:

- PASS: A first-time visitor can identify the recommended product path immediately after the hero.
- PASS: The page moves from brand message to product decision without confusion.
- FAIL: The page still requires interpretation before the visitor knows what to buy.

## 10. Sprint Success Definition

Success does not require conversion metrics, revenue claims, or sales guarantees.

The sprint is successful when:

- product value appears earlier on the homepage
- the buying path becomes clearer
- trust appears closer to the first decision point
- placeholder or weak early product presentation is corrected
- before/after difference is visible in desktop screenshots
- before/after difference is visible in mobile screenshots
- the merchant can understand what changed without needing a technical explanation

## 11. Implementation Readiness Decision

Decision: Conditional Go.

Why:

The after state is clear enough to implement, and the selected sprint is focused enough for a $950 ShopiFixer Fix Sprint. The existing evidence supports the need for a clearer homepage buying path.

Conditions before implementation:

- Shopify theme access is available.
- The primary product or collection is confirmed.
- The selected product has a real image.
- The selected product has the correct title.
- The selected product has the correct price.
- The selected product or collection link works.
- Trust statements are verified and approved.

If these conditions are met, the sprint is ready to execute.
