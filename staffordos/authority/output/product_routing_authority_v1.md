# PRODUCT ROUTING AUTHORITY V1

## Purpose

Determine which Stafford Media offering should be presented to a merchant.

Products are selected based on observed business problems.

Do not default to a product.

Route based on evidence.

---

## Entry Point

Lead
 ->
Qualification
 ->
Product Routing

---

## Route 1: ShopiFixer

Use when:

- visible UX friction exists
- product page friction exists
- cart friction exists
- checkout friction exists
- trust issues are visible
- navigation problems are visible
- one scoped sprint can improve experience

Primary Offer:

ShopiFixer Fix Sprint

Price:

$950 flat fee

Deliverables:

- issue identified
- issue fixed
- before evidence
- after evidence
- proof package

Success Metric:

merchant receives visible improvement and proof

---

## Route 2: Abando

Use when:

- cart abandonment appears significant
- merchant has traffic already
- checkout exists
- recovery automation is missing
- recovery messaging is weak
- merchant wants ongoing recovery

Primary Offer:

Abando

Deliverables:

- install
- abandoned cart detection
- recovery flow
- attribution reporting

Success Metric:

recovered revenue

---

## Route 3: Consulting

Use when:

- issue is broader than one sprint
- merchant needs strategy
- merchant needs systems guidance
- merchant needs marketing operations support
- merchant needs transformation planning

Primary Offer:

Consulting Engagement

Success Metric:

business objective achieved

---

## Route 4: Disqualify

Disqualify when:

- no visible opportunity
- no authority to buy
- no realistic budget
- no meaningful traffic
- issue cannot be scoped

Success Metric:

avoid wasting fulfillment capacity

## Operator Rule

Every merchant must have:

- lifecycle stage
- selected product
- packet status
- fulfillment status
- proof status

visible from StaffordOS Command Center.

---

## Anti-Drift Rule

Do not create separate operating systems for:

- ShopiFixer
- Abando
- Consulting

Use one StaffordOS lifecycle with multiple product routes.
